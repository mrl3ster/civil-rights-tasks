Separate is Never Equal

A Reading Task on Summarizing

I can summarize the main events of a story in a short, clear way.

📚 Author's Craft

  • A summary tells the most important parts of a story in just a few sentences.
  • Leave out small details. Keep only what matters.
  • Ask: What happened? Why did it happen? What changed?
  • A good summary is short but tells the whole story.
Why this story matters: The Civil War (1861-1865) tore the United States apart. It was fought over slavery and whether states could leave the country. It killed more Americans than any other war. Understanding why it happened helps us understand America today.

📖 Reading Passage

The Civil War: A Country Breaks Apart

By 1860, America was splitting apart. Southern states wanted to keep slavery. Northern states wanted to end it. They couldn't agree. Abraham Lincoln became president, and the South was furious. South Carolina left the United States. Then other Southern states left too. They created their own country called the Confederate States of America. The North said, "You can't leave." The South said, "We can." On April 12, 1861, Confederate soldiers fired on a Northern fort called Fort Sumter. The Civil War had begun.

For four terrible years, Americans fought Americans. Brother fought brother. Father fought son. Soldiers died in battles at places like Gettysburg and Antietam. Thousands of people died from disease. Families were torn apart. Cities were destroyed. Farms were burned. The war was the deadliest in American history. More than 600,000 people died.

In 1865, the North finally won. The South surrendered. The United States was one country again. Slavery was ended. But the cost was huge. The nation was broken. People on both sides had lost loved ones. The South was destroyed. It would take decades for America to heal. The Civil War changed America forever. It showed that the country was worth fighting for, but at a terrible price.
