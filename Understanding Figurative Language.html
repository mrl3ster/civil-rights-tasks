📚 Figurative Language

  • Figurative language uses words in unexpected ways to create meaning and feeling.
  • Sound devices include alliteration, assonance, and onomatopoeia.
  • Sensory words help readers see, hear, taste, smell, and feel.
  • Idioms have meanings different from the literal words.
  • Puns are plays on words that use double meanings for humor.
Why this matters: Authors use figurative language to make writing more interesting and to help readers connect emotionally with the text. Understanding these techniques helps you appreciate how powerful writing can be.
